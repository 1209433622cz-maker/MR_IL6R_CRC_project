## Core result to add

Manual GEO phenotype validation included 1,107 samples across GSE14333, GSE17536, GSE17537 and GSE39582. In GSE39582, IL6R expression was significantly higher in non-tumoral colorectal mucosa than in tumour samples (median 6.677 vs 4.064; Wilcoxon P = 3.94 x 10^-13). Stage-stratified analyses across four GEO cohorts were non-significant, and univariable Cox models did not support IL6R as a robust standalone prognostic marker.

## Recommended interpretation

These results strengthen the current conservative main line: IL6R is not supported as a circulating-protein causal risk factor, shared-variant CRC-risk signal, tumour-intrinsic upregulated oncogene, or robust standalone prognostic marker. Instead, the data support an immune-, myeloid- and inflammatory-associated tumour microenvironment interpretation.
